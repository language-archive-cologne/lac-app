The Language Archive Cologne (LAC) maintains a formal preservation policy to ensure sustainable long-term access to digital language materials.

Core Mission

LAC commits to preserving digital audio and video data, annotations, and other digital language data in the long term through active preservation strategies rather than passive storage.

Preservation Structure

Governance

Archive management oversees preservation planning in collaboration with the Data Centre for the Humanities and the Department of Linguistics.

Quality Assurance

Automated integrity checks occur quarterly as part of standard workflow to verify data viability.

Two-Tier Preservation System

Level 1 (Full Support)

The highest tier guarantees complete functionality including file integrity, displayability, future intelligibility, and format migration. Monitored formats include:

  • Audio: WAV (LPCM)
  • Video: MPEG-4 Part 14 and QuickTime formats
  • Annotations: ELAN files
  • Other: PDF/A and UTF-8 text

Level 0 (Best Effort)

This tier maintains file integrity and intelligibility through metadata provision, persistent identifiers, checksums, backup copies, and periodic media refreshment - without guaranteed format migration. Supported formats include:

  • Annotation standards: Praat, Exmaralda, TEI
  • Image types: TIFF, JPEG2000, PNG
  • Tabular data
