The Language Archive Cologne's depositing policy outlines our commitment to long-term preservation and dissemination of digital language resources.

Scope and Mission

The archive has an explicit mission to preserve digital audio and video data, annotations, and other digital language data in the long term. We serve researchers, institutions, and individuals globally, with particular focus on regions lacking local archival support.

Eligibility

Services are available to any researchers, institutions and individuals - explicitly including students and native speakers - worldwide. This inclusive approach extends to endangered language documentation projects.

Support Services

The archive provides assistance across the research lifecycle, from initial planning through project completion. We offer guidance on methodological and technical aspects including equipment selection, digital tools, format standards, and metadata creation.

Engagement Process

Organizations with active or planned projects should contact the LAC helpdesk early on in the project, preferably in the planning phase. Those with existing legacy language data are encouraged to reach out.

Referral Support

The archive commits to helping depositors locate alternative institutions if LAC proves unsuitable for their specific materials.
