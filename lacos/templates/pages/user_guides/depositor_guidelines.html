Becoming a Depositor

Prospective depositors must obtain collection owner approval or establish new collections through the LAC-Helpdesk. Signing the LAC Depositor Agreement is required, along with providing information about data types, volume of data, and legal situation of the data.

Ethical and Rights Considerations

Depositors must hold rights to their data or have permission from rights holders. The archive addresses sensitive materials through restricted access metadata storage and, when necessary, offline preservation on TSM systems to prevent accidental exposure of confidential information.

Structural Organization

The system uses three hierarchical levels:

  • Collections - Shared origin or topic
  • Bundles - Single recorded events with metadata
  • Files - Individual audio, video, or annotation documents

We recommend keeping bundles simple in structure to avoid redundancy.

Submission Process

Depositors should follow the Submission Guidelines to prepare materials. The LAC currently lacks a self-service deposit interface, requiring users to arrange the transfer of submission-ready data directly with staff after compliance checks.

Support

The LAC-Helpdesk provides ongoing guidance for unclear situations, format questions, and collection organization decisions.
